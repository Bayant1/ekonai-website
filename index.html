What is MMAT?

UNESCO Priority UNESCO Language MMAT Response
Human Rights & Dignity Ensure respect for human dignity, freedom, and cultural diversity. MMAT centers Nirvair (non-hostility) and Ik Onkar (unity) as constraints against dehumanizing outputs.
Transparency & Explainability Promote transparency and explainability of AI systems. MMAT uses JSON-LD ethical metadata + public scoring rubrics (e.g., Sat Naam rubric) across all models.
Accountability Establish accountability mechanisms across the AI lifecycle. MMAT is cross-model scoring, discrepancy flags, and alignment audits per principle.
Ethical & Inclusive Design Ensure ethical impact assessments and inclusive design methods. MMAT is co-designed with spiritual frameworks and tested across multicultural, multilingual dilemmas.

The Mool Mantar Alignment Test (MMAT) is a benchmark for AI outputs, grounded in Sikh spiritual values like Nirbhau (Fearlessness) and Sat Naam (Truth). Instead of just avoiding harm, MMAT scores whether an AI upholds ethical clarity, timelessness, and inner integrity. The protocol is open-source, tested across four AI systems, and freely available on GitHub.
